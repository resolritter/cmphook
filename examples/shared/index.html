What is this about?

The above app shows a parent which hosts a "tick" and "tock" states. The Ticker childs are interested in the tick increases, while the Tocker childs are interested in the Tock increases.

Since Hooker is framework-agnostic, its state management approach is not bound to the frameworks' lifecycles runtime invariants, thus the updates can be finely controlled. For instance, even though the parent is managing two child's states (the tick and the tock), the children will only update when their state of interest changes. In comparison, most frameworks will diff the whole tree on any state change.


Notice how:

  • How Parent is not updating, since it isn't consuming the data itself, only hosting the context.
  • Multiple "Tick" children depending on the same "prop".
